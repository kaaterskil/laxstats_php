NEW GAME SETUP

Creating a new game means entering in some basic information before you get to the good stuff, the stats, that is:

  1. Game Date: Laxstats needs to know a game date so that it can apply the correct roster of players. But if you're entering your season schedule well before it happens, you might not know for sure the date or time. That's OK: Enter the date as best you know it - you can change it later if you need to so everyone stays current. To enter a date, type slashes and a four-digit year, e.g. mm/dd/yyyy.
  2. Start time: Same deal here - Enter a playng time as best you know it. You can change it later if you need to. There are two ways to enter a time:
    • Using a 24-hour clock, type the hours and minutes separated by a colon, e.g. hh:mm.
    • Using a 12-hour clock, type the hours and minutes separated by a colon, adding "am" or "pm" with a space after the time, e.g. 4:00 pm. Note that there are no periods in "am" or "pm".
  3. Playing Field: Select a field from the drop-down menu. If you don't see your field listed, then you've got exit this area, create your field, and then come back. Sorry, dude, but that's how it goes.
  4. Home team: Select a team from the drop-down menu. If you don't see the correct team, then you've got to exit this area, create the team, and then come back. Doh!
  5. Visiting team: Same deal as for the home team.
  6. Season: Check this box if the game is a post-season game. Leave it blank for a regular season game.
  7. Game Type: Check this box if the game is an exhibition or non-league game. Leave it blank for all league games. NOTE: Every game is added to your win-loss record, so don't enter scrimmages or practice games.
  8. Weather: Obviously, you can't enter information here until the game happens.
  9. Referee, Umpire and Field Judge, Scorekeeper, Timekeeper: If you want to track them, put something down. Otherwise, fuggedaboutit!
To save your work, hit the Save button. You must have at least the required information (identified with a red asterisk) before a game can be saved. If you need to start over, hit the Reset button.

Hopefully, Laxstats is a smart system. It will report back to you if there's a problem with your entry, like if you're duplicating a game you've already entered or if you've typed in some bad data. You can't miss the notes when they happen - they're in embarrassingly bright red.

Once you save your game, Laxstats takes you to a new screen where you can start entering game results. If you're not ready to do that yet, return to the Manager's Office or go anywhere else you'd like. Once you've entered your data, checked it for accuracy and are perfectly happy with it, go back to the manager's office and submit it as Final. Careful though - once a game is submitted as Final, it's set in stone and you cannot retrieve it for modification.

PLAY INPUT

While you and your opponents have rosters, that doesn't mean that everyone got the chance to play. Here is where you designate all the game's players, identify your starters and substitutes, and enter their shots and ground balls. You'll need to enter everyone individually:

  1. Home/Visitor: Click on the appropriate radio button. The drop down menu you'll need in the next step will be loaded with the appropriate roster.
  2. Player: Select the player from the drop-down menu.
  3. Position: We've given you the opportunity to move a player around (from Midfield to Attack, say). Otherwise, the position you identified when you set the player up in the roster will be automatically entered.
  4. Status: If the player played int he game, click the "Played" checkbox. If the player was a starter, check both "Played" and "Started".
  5. Shots: Enter the number of shots for the player. If you don't track shots by quarter, enter the information in the first box. Shots are automatically totalled so you can check your data entry for accuracy.
  6. Ground Balls: Same deal: Enter the number of ground balls for the player. If you don't track by quarter, enter the information in the first box.

PENALTY INPUT

Tracking penalties can be tricky - Was there a man-up goal that occured? Was the player released when the opponent scored? Laxstats tries to make it easy by figuring all that out for you. It not only computes Man-Up/Man-Down activity, it organizes penalties by categories that can be analyzed later for trends. To create an entry:

  1. Home/Visitor: Click on the appropriate radio button. The drop down menu you'll need in the next step will be loaded with the appropriate roster.
  2. Player: Select the player from the drop-down menu.
  3. Quarter: Enter the quarter when the penalty occurred. (For overtime penalties, enter OT, O2, O3, etc. This is important.)
  4. Clock: Enter the time remaining on the clock. Laxstats will carry forward any unreleased penalties into the following period.
  5. Infraction: Choose a violation from the drop-down menu. For conduct fouls, choose whether the violation resulted in a lock-down or releasable penalty.
  6. Duration: Enter the penalty duration in minutes, e.g. 0.5 for 30 seconds, 1 for a minute, etc.

If you make a mistake, you can reset the form by hitting the Reset button. When you're done with the entry, hit Save and move on the the next penalty.

PLAYBOOK

NOTE: Your browser must be Javascript/Java-enabled in order to load and save plays. Also, many Macintosh browsers and browsers running on Windows 3.1 (among others) don't communicate well with plug-ins like this animation, so loading plays from the server could be a bit spotty. Loading a Saved Play: Select a play from the pull-down menu.

If you have designed and saved a game and you don't see it in the drop-down menu, you may need to refresh your screen.

Creating a New Play:

  1. Draw a path for a player to travel: Click a Path button and use your mouse to draw a line in the direction you want your player to travel. The path stops when you lift up on the mouse. You can draw any combination of paths - the program will snap a new segment onto the end of the previous one if you click your mouse near it. Finish off by terminating your path with a Tee or an Arrow end. (Select the line first by clicking on it to highlight it blue, then click on one of the End buttons.)
  2. Create a player: Click a Player button and drag your player icon over to the path you drew.
  3. Link the player to the path: Get your player icon to touch any end or corner in the path, and click the Link button when the dialog box appears. The icon will snap to the starting point. (If you make a mistake, select the icon or the path to highlight it blue, then click the Clear button to erase.)
  4. Set your player's timeline. All players' movements are adjustable in time. Double-click a player icon to bring up the timeline bar. You have a maximium of 10 seconds for your play. Drag the edges of the bars left or right to either delay start, shorten the play (speed up the runner), or have your player wait between path segments.
  5. Add more players: Add as many players and pathways as you want.
  6. Add the ball: Select a player by clicking on it and click the Ball button.
  7. Add pass and shoot paths: Click the Line button under Balls and draw paths to pass or shoot. Place your mouse near a node on a player's path and then move it to another player's path node or to the goal. The pass/shoot path will snap into place.
  8. Adjust timelines: Make sure your player will be there to catch the ball when it gets passed or it will get thrown away! Double-click a player icon to bring up the timeline again.
  9. Run the play: Press Run to see the play in action. Reset brings everyone back to their original position.
  10. Save it! If you have at least one player and a travel path, you can save your play by clicking the Save Play button. Enter a title for your play and some brief comments. Rest assured that your play can ONLY be viewed from this team page.

There are many ways you can optimize your images to stay within this limit. However, most of them may require the use of some sort of image editing software, such as Adobe Photoshop, Adobe ImageReady, etc. or a drawing program.

Using your editing software, you can usually find a balance between image size and resolution. Typically 800 x 534 pixels is a good size. And, given that this is not ESPN's website, medium quality resolution should suffice. Obviously, smaller images at higher resolution will work too, but we're not sure if you really want to see individual sweat droplets spraying off somebody as they're being checked. Then again...
